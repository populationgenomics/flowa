## Abstract

We review clinical outcomes in a cohort of F508del homozygous patients
followed for ten years, with particular attention to pancreatic
phenotype and response to triple-modulator therapy.

## Cohort

The cohort comprised individuals with confirmed F508del genotypes,
recruited from cystic fibrosis clinics across multiple sites. Ascertainment
was via newborn screening and clinical referral.

## Pancreatic phenotype

Homozygous F508del patients exhibit pancreatic insufficiency in
approximately 92% of cases, requiring lifelong pancreatic enzyme
replacement therapy. Insufficiency was diagnosed by faecal elastase or
pancreatic stimulation testing.

## Modulator response

Elexacaftor-tezacaftor-ivacaftor combination therapy restores partial
CFTR function as measured by sweat chloride reduction and percent
predicted FEV1 improvement. Approximately 85% of treated F508del
homozygotes show a clinically meaningful response. Sweat chloride
typically falls by 40–50 mmol/L from baseline.

## Discussion

The clinical findings are consistent with the cellular mechanism in
which F508del CFTR is retained in the ER and cleared by ERAD. Modulators
that improve ER folding rescue a fraction of the protein, restore some
channel activity at the cell surface, and improve disease markers.
